The Effect of Cannabis Legalization on the Frequency of Hospitalizations for Cannabis Hyperemesis Syndrome (with Isabel Hujoel and Joseph Murray)

We performed a comparative analysis on four states with different cannabis policies between 2010 and 2015 using a Difference-in-Differences study design that controls for time and state-specific heterogeneity. The treated states were Washington (legalized recreational in 2012) and Maryland (legalized medical in 2013), and the control states were Arizona and Kentucky, whose cannabis policies were invariant during this time (legalized medical in Arizona, no legalized cannabis in Kentucky). We used the State Inpatient Database which includes all inpatient discharges from community hospitals. Admissions for CHS were identified as those with a primary diagnosis of vomiting and an additional diagnosis of cannabis abuse/use.
